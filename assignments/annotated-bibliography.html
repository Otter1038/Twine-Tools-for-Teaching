Annotated
Bibliography

DueSunday, June 21 — 11:59 PM
Points10 pts

Purpose

An annotated bibliography is a research tool that helps you organize, evaluate, and document your sources. Each citation is followed by an annotation that summarizes the source and explains why it is credible and relevant to your project. This assignment asks you to think strategically about the sources you have chosen and to begin mapping connections between them.

★
Use the Interactive Tool First Before you begin, work through the Annotated Bibliography Guide to understand how to evaluate sources and structure your annotations effectively.

What to Submit

A brief introduction (one-half to one page) describing your research project and research question, followed by an alphabetically ordered annotated bibliography of six credible, relevant secondary sources. Two of these sources must be peer-reviewed journal articles.

Source Requirements

Minimum Standards

  • Minimum 6 sources total
  • At least 2 must be peer-reviewed journal articles
  • All sources must be credible and directly relevant to your research question

Citation & Annotation Format

Format all citations in MLA or APA style. Each annotation should be 200–250 words in two paragraphs:

¶1
Summary. Summarize the main relevant points of the source — what is the central argument, what evidence does the author use, and what conclusions do they reach?
¶2
Evaluation & Relevance. Explain how the source is credible, relevant, and useful to your project. Discuss how it connects to your research question and to other sources in your bibliography.
